Community Impact
Beyond the balance sheet

This project exists because the costs of AI infrastructure are not evenly distributed. The companies building and operating data centers in Virginia capture enormous economic value — but the grid stress, rate increases, and environmental externalities are shared broadly, and felt most acutely by people with the least capacity to absorb them.

Methodology & Transparency

Global counters: Live estimates use the following annual baselines divided to per-second rates: Energy ~1,000 TWh/yr (IEA 2026 projection); Water ~10.2T L/yr (UC Riverside extrapolated); CO₂ ~895M kg/yr (IEA grid intensity × energy); Prompts ~44T/yr (Goldman Sachs extrapolated). All figures have wide uncertainty ranges (±30–60%).

Prompt calculator: Per-token estimates from Luccioni et al. (2023). Energy: 0.001–0.01 Wh/query. Water: 10–60 mL/query. CO₂: 0.3–3g/query. Complexity estimated from token count. Confidence: Low (±50%).

Company estimates: Derived from company sustainability reports, IEA data, Goldman Sachs analyst reports, and extrapolation. AI-specific energy not separately disclosed by most companies. Confidence: Low–Med (30–55%).

Virginia EIA data: Queries EIA Open Data API endpoint electricity/retail-sales with stateid=VA§orid=COM. Free key at eia.gov/opendata. Add to js/data.js.

Independence: Independent student research project. No affiliation with Dominion Energy, PJM, any data center operator, or AI company. All data from public sources.
